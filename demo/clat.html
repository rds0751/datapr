CLAT

CLAT stands for Common Law Admission Test. It is the topmost law entrance examination conducted at a national level.
The common Law Admission test is conducted by 19 law schools every year for graduate/undergraduate programmes. The students are selected on the basis of merit-cum-prefernece.
For LL.B program qualification is Intermediate with 45%.For LLM programs only Law graduates can apply.

Eligibility:

  • Pass in 10+2 with 45% marks (Gen/Obc), 40% for SC/ST Categories.
  • Candidates who are appearing in the qualifying examination in March/April 2018 are also eligible for appearing in CLAT-2018 online examination.

Highlights of CLAT 2019

This is an Natioanl level exam which is conducted every year. You can easily fill up the form of CLAT by reaching www.clat.ac.in. Application Mode is Online only. Exam Mode of CLAT is Offline i.e. Pen & Paper

Age Limit:

For Under Graduate Program, As per revised rules, there is no upper age limit for CLAT 2019.

CLAT Exam Pattern

The national law entrance exam will be of two hours duration. The offline mode exam will compromise of 200 objective type questions.

Here are some Highlights of CLAT exam pattern:

There are 5 sections in Exam. Mode of examination is Offline and Objective type.
Total Questions: 200

1. English/Verbal Ability- Total 40 question
2. Quantitative Aptitude – Total 20 question
3. Logical Reasoning – Total 40 question
4. General Awareness – Total 50 question
5. Legal Aptitude – Total 50 question
